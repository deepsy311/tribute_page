Dr APJ Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Here’s a concise life history of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, highlighted in 20 key points, along with a testimonial from another prominent leader:

  1. Birth and Early Life - Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a modest Tamil Muslim family.
  2. Family Background - His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner, and his mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife. He had four siblings.
  3. Education - Attended Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram and later graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  4. Aspirations in Engineering - Initially wanted to become a pilot, but later pursued aerospace engineering.
  5. Higher Education - Studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).
  6. Early Career - Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist after graduation.
  7. ISRO Career - Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1969, where he was the project director for India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III).
  8. SLV-III Success - Successfully launched the Rohini satellite into near-Earth orbit in July 1980, marking India’s entry into the space club.
  9. Missile Man of India - Led the development of India's missile programs, including Agni and Prithvi, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India."
  10. Leadership at DRDO - Became the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of DRDO, leading the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program.
  11. Pokhran II Tests - Played a crucial role in the 1998 nuclear tests, which established India as a nuclear power.
  12. Awards and Honors - Received numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna in 1997, India's highest civilian award.
  13. Authorship - Wrote several books, including his autobiography, "Wings of Fire," inspiring millions.
  14. Presidency - Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, known for his simplicity, humility, and visionary ideas.
  15. People’s President - Popular among all age groups, especially the youth, and known for his accessibility and interaction with the common man.
  16. Post-Presidency - Continued to engage in educational and motivational activities, focusing on inspiring the youth of India.
  17. Vision 2020 - Advocated for the Vision 2020 plan, aiming to transform India into a developed nation by 2020.
  18. Educational Initiatives - Worked extensively in the field of education, emphasizing the importance of science and technology for national development.
  19. Final Moments - Passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) Shillong.
  20. Legacy - Left behind a legacy of scientific achievement, integrity, and inspiration, remembered as a true visionary and a beloved figure in India and beyond.

"Dr. Kalam’s distinguished service and immense contributions to our nation have always inspired us. He was a rare example of humility and greatness combined in one personality. His vision and efforts towards the empowerment of the youth and the development of the nation have left an indelible mark on our society. He was not just a scientist and a statesman but also a mentor to the youth of India, always advocating for the nation's progress through education and innovation. His life and work will continue to inspire future generations to dream and strive for a better India."

- Dr. Manmohan Singh, Former Prime Minister of India
